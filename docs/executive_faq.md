# Executive FAQ

## What exactly is Nova?

Nova is a third-party platform that modernizes the **Ingenium** life-insurance core (legacy COBOL on AIX). It integrates with your existing system and upgrades it in stages — it does not replace or modify your Ingenium licence or source. You keep your business logic, data, and full IP ownership.

## Has this been done before?

Yes. Nova is proven in production from a **real AIX-to-Linux migration completed in ~9 months**, where deployment time for a representative change moved from around **3 hours to about 20 minutes** with a planned downtime window of about **5 minutes** per deployment. (Client details are confidential; we can walk through an anonymized case under NDA.)

## Is this a full core replacement?

No. Nova is staged modernization. It protects your existing Ingenium logic and gives you a controlled path forward, one phase at a time, with rollback at each step.

## Do we have to move to the cloud?

No. The Foundation phase moves Ingenium from AIX to **Linux**, which can be on-premise, hybrid, or cloud — your choice.

## How quickly can we see value?

A 6-week, Windows-first POC can demonstrate the build-and-deploy improvement and produce a board-ready evidence pack, followed by an included 6-month trial period for extended evaluation.

## What is the risk compared to big-bang programs?

Much lower. Nova isolates scope, proves value early on your own system, and keeps every step reversible — instead of a multi-year all-or-nothing replacement.

## What should be in the first board update?

- The before/after build-and-deploy baseline from the POC
- POC scope and governance model
- Decision gates and investment scenarios for the next phase
- Risks, dependencies, and mitigation ownership
