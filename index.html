What Nova is, in plain terms

If you run Ingenium, here is exactly what this is, the problem it solves, and where you start.

What Nova is

A third-party modernization platform for insurers on the Ingenium life core. It integrates with and gradually upgrades your existing system — it does not replace or touch your Ingenium licence or source.

The problem it fixes

Legacy COBOL on AIX with hour-long compiles, manual deployments, no REST APIs, and a shrinking COBOL talent pool — all slowing product launches and raising run-cost.

What you get first

Nexus: a VS Code toolchain that automates COBOL builds, deployments, and environment management. Hours become minutes, and COBOL becomes AI-assisted inside a modern IDE.

Why it is credible

Built from a real Ingenium-to-cloud migration completed in ~9 months. You keep full IP ownership; deploy on cloud, on-prem, or hybrid. No vendor lock-in.

The problems Ingenium teams face every day

These slow product launches, raise run-cost, and increase operational risk.

COBOL builds take hours

Small changes trigger long compiles. Releases are slow, and engineers wait instead of shipping.

AIX is expensive & aging

Proprietary AIX, IBM MQ and WebSphere carry high licence and maintenance cost, and the COBOL talent pool keeps shrinking.

Manual, risky deployments

Releases and environment setup are manual and drift over time — the same test passes in one environment and fails in another.

No modern APIs

Without REST endpoints, connecting to digital channels, bancassurance partners, and new products needs fragile workarounds.

The benefits, in concrete terms

Every benefit maps to a specific operational change — not a slogan.

  1. Reduce deployment time from around 3 hours to around 20 minutes with automated COBOL builds and one-click releases in VS Code.
  2. Reduce legacy platform dependency by moving from AIX/MQ/WebSphere toward open, Linux-based infrastructure (cloud optional).
  3. Open your core with REST APIs so digital channels and partners integrate without MQ middleware.
  4. Preserve your Ingenium business logic and data — modernize around it, phase by phase, with rollback at every step.
